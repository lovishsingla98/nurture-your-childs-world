# Topic Research Methodology

Follow these 5 steps in order during every content session to identify the best topic to write about. Do not skip steps.

---

## Step 1 — Reddit Research

Search these subreddits for posts from the last 7 days:

- r/Parenting
- r/Mommit
- r/daddit
- r/parentsofmultiples

**What to look for:**
- Top posts by upvote count (sort by "Top — Past Week")
- Recurring questions, complaints, or anxieties
- Posts with high upvotes AND high comment counts — this signals strong emotional resonance, which correlates with search demand
- Specific ages mentioned (prioritise posts about ages 3–12)

**Output:** List 5–8 candidate topics with the Reddit signal that inspired them.

---

## Step 2 — Google Trends Signals

Search Google Trends for queries related to the candidate topics from Step 1.

**What to look for:**
- Rising queries in parenting / child development / education
- Seasonal trends relevant to the current month (e.g. "back to school anxiety" in August, "summer activities for kids" in May)
- Queries with "Breakout" status (>5000% increase) — these are emerging topics with low competition

**Output:** Note which candidate topics have Trends support and any new topics surfaced by Trends.

---

## Step 3 — Competitor Content Gaps

Check recent content from these competitors:

- **Aha Parenting** (ahaparenting.com)
- **Big Life Journal** (biglifejournal.com/blogs)
- **What To Expect** (whattoexpect.com)
- **Zero to Three** (zerotothree.org)
- **Understood.org** (understood.org)

**What to look for:**
- Topics they cover shallowly (< 600 words) or only as listicles
- Topics with outdated information (published > 2 years ago, no updates)
- Topics they don't cover at all for the 5–12 age range
- Top-ranking Google results that are thin, generic, or AI-slop

**Output:** Flag candidate topics where we can produce clearly better content than what currently ranks.

---

## Step 4 — Keyword Intent Classification

For each candidate topic still in the running, classify the user's search intent:

| Intent | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Informational** (how, what, why, when) | Proceed — ideal for blog content |
| **Navigational** (brand name, specific site) | Skip |
| **Transactional** (buy, price, best product) | Skip for now |
| **Commercial investigation** (review, comparison) | Skip for now |

Only informational-intent topics move forward to scoring.

---

## Step 5 — Shortlist Scoring

Score each remaining candidate on these criteria:

| Criterion | Scale | What 5 means |
|-----------|-------|--------------|
| **Search volume signal** | 1–5 | High Reddit upvotes + Google Trends support |
| **Competition level** | 1–5 | Existing top content is thin, outdated, or generic (5 = weak competition) |
| **Brand fit** | 1–5 | Directly relevant to ages 3–12 and our content domains |
| **Freshness** | Pass/Fail | Not already in `published-topics.md` |

**Scoring table format:**

| # | Topic | Search Signal | Competition | Brand Fit | Fresh? | Total |
|---|-------|--------------|-------------|-----------|--------|-------|
| 1 | ... | 4 | 5 | 5 | Pass | 14 |
| 2 | ... | 3 | 4 | 5 | Pass | 12 |

**Output:** Present the top 5 topics ranked by total score. Recommend #1 as the topic to write, with a one-sentence justification.
